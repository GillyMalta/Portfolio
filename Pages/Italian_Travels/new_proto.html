An introduction some Italian travels. Our blog:

My wife Sylvana and I love food, especially Italian food. Italy is our preferred destination not only for the close proximity to Malta, but because we love their culture, landscapes and the people. For me personally, Italian super cars, exotics, hyper and not.. and the automotive legacy... in particular, Ferrari... more directly, the man behind the brand... Enzo Ferrari!

Italy is a country of unparalleled charm, blending a rich history with world-class cuisine, iconic fashion, and breathtaking landscapes. Its cultural heritage is evident at every turn, from the grandeur of ancient Roman architecture in the Colosseum and the Pantheon to the artistic treasures of the Renaissance showcased in Florence and Venice. Italy's regions each offer their own unique appeal: the romantic waterways of Venice, the stunning coastline of the Amalfi, and the rolling vineyards of Tuscany are but a few of the attractions that draw visitors from around the globe. Steeped in tradition, Italy is also home to a vibrant culture where festivals, music, and warm hospitality create an unforgettable experience.

Italian cuisine is one of the country's greatest gifts to the world, emphasising fresh, quality ingredients and simple yet exquisite flavours. From freshly made pasta and creamy risottos to wood-fired pizzas and indulgent gelatos, every meal feels like an authentic celebration of life. Equally renowned is Italy’s influence on global fashion. Visiting Italy isn’t just about the sights and tastes; it’s about immersing yourself in a way of life that blends the old and new effortlessly. With every city offering something distinctive, from art and history to haute couture and gastronomy, Italy is truly a destination that inspires all who visit.

Enzo Ferrari. The man, the legend:


Enzo Ferrari, born in 1898 in Modena, Italy, was a passionate racing enthusiast who founded Scuderia Ferrari in 1929 as a racing team for Alfa Romeo. In 1947, after parting ways with Alfa Romeo, he established Ferrari S.p.A. and introduced the first Ferrari-badged car, the 125 S, powered by a V12 engine—a symbol of Ferrari's engineering brilliance and dedication to performance.

Ferrari quickly gained fame for its dominance in motorsport, particularly in Formula One, becoming the most successful team in the sport’s history. Over the decades, the company also evolved into a luxury carmaker, crafting high-performance road cars that combined cutting-edge technology with Italian artistry.

Today, Ferrari remains one of the most prestigious car brands in the world, with its headquarters in Maranello, Italy. It continues to innovate in both motorsport and luxury vehicles, integrating hybrid technology with models such as the SF90 Stradale and embracing sustainability while preserving its tradition of excellence. Beyond cars, Ferrari has expanded its global influence with iconic museums. The Museo Ferrari in Maranello celebrates the company’s history in motorsport and engineering, while the Enzo Ferrari Museum in Modena offers an in-depth look at the founder’s life and the origins of the Ferrari legacy.

Ferrari’s legacy is defined by its relentless pursuit of speed, innovation, and style, epitomising the pinnacle of automotive excellence and inspiring a global passion for high-performance cars. Its museums attract enthusiasts from around the world, serving as a testament to its cultural and automotive impact.

The Rosso Ferrari or Ferrari Red iconic status!

Rosso Ferrari, often referred to simply as "Ferrari Red," is one of the most iconic colours in automotive history. This vibrant shade of red has been synonymous with the Ferrari brand since its inception, symbolising the passion, performance, and prestige associated with the marque. The origin of the colour can be traced back to the early days of motor racing when international racing colours were assigned to different countries, with Italy adopting red. Ferrari, as an Italian car manufacturer, proudly carried this heritage into its identity, ensuring that Rosso Ferrari would become a hallmark of their cars.

The hue itself is characterised by its rich, deep red tone with a slight hint of warmth, making it both striking and timeless. Over the years, Ferrari has introduced variations of the colour, such as Rosso Corsa, Rosso Scuderia, and Rosso Dino, allowing customers to personalise their vehicles while still embracing the brand's legacy. Whether gracing the sleek curves of a classic Ferrari 250 GTO or the aerodynamic bodywork of a modern-day SF90 Stradale, Rosso Ferrari captures the essence of speed, elegance, and craftsmanship that defines the Ferrari brand. This legendary shade continues to evoke excitement and admiration, a true representation of Italian design and engineering excellence.
